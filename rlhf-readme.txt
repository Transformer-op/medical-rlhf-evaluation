Medical AI RLHF Evaluation Project

What this project is

A human evaluation project simulating the RLHF (Reinforcement Learning
from Human Feedback) annotation workflow used by medical AI companies
including Outlier, Scale AI, and Appen. The evaluator reviews pairs of
AI-generated responses to medical questions and selects the better one
using a structured clinical rubric.

How the evaluation works

Each of the 20 questions has two AI-generated responses — Response A
and Response B. Both are scored on five criteria worth 5 points each
for a maximum of 25 points per response:


Clinical Accuracy
Safety
Completeness
Clarity
Appropriate Referral


The response with the higher total score is selected as the winner.
All decisions include written reasoning grounded in clinical knowledge.

Key findings from this evaluation


Response B was the weaker response in all 20 questions
The most common failure was dangerous reassurance — minimising
serious symptoms to make the patient feel comfortable
Safety was the most critical criterion — a response that is 90%
correct but includes one dangerous piece of advice is worse than
a shorter response that is fully safe
Clinical precision (specific numbers, thresholds, named conditions)
consistently separated strong responses from weak ones
Appropriate referral to professional care was missing in most
of the weaker responses
